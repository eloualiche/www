Erik Loualiche
Associate Professor of Finance · Carlson School of Management · University of Minnesota

I am an Associate Professor of Finance at the Carlson School of Management, University of Minnesota.

My research is on asset pricing, with a focus on globalization, entry and competition, and the role of risk in shaping the cross-section of returns. I received my PhD from MIT Sloan.

My current work studies causal inference in asset pricing, exchange-rate transmission of monetary policy, and the q-theory of local governments.
